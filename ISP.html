Clean energy car conversions

In 11th grade, I did an independant study project on converting gasoline cars to run with less emissions

A quick synopsis

Humans are destroying the planet slowly with our extreme carbon emissions from power and transportation, the result being global warming. My study was about creating low-cost, environmentally friendly, transportation to help solve this problem. My procedure was to first research different types of fuel, their manufacturing, and their carbon impact. From that research, I extrapolated which fuels would be the easiest to use in a biofuel conversation, also the cheapest to implement. That fuel was ethanol, which possessed very similar physical and chemical properties to gasoline. These similarities are very important for easy, quick and cheap implementation of the fuel. Once the fuel type was identified, I then proceeded to modify a Stihl S10 chainsaw to run on ethanol to prove that the necessary modifications were very easy. I was able to do so for less than $50, proving that ethanol is a good substitute for gasoline. One problem did arise during this research project, that being the production of the ethanol. Because it is produced from plants, typically corn or sugarcane, it would require a large amount of those respective plants to produce enough ethanol to replace gasoline entirely. In order for a full systematic replacement, not just in my engine, but in all engines, to happen ethanol production would need to become much more efficient otherwise the carbon cost-benefits are negligible. Finally once all the research and modifications were done, it was time to run the engine. It ran successfully for 20 minutes, fulfilling my criteria, making my research project a success.
